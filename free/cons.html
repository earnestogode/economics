Let's explore what some of the arguments are against trade protection

Misallocation of resources
  • Protectionist measures can distort market prices and disrupt the optimal allocation of resources
  • By artificially inflating the cost of imports or lowering the cost of domestic goods, protectionism can lead consumers to make purchases that do not reflect true market demand
  • This can result in resources being diverted from more efficient uses to less efficient
Retaliation
  • Implementing tariffs can provoke trading partners to retaliate, potentially sparking trade wars that escalate tensions and harm economic relations
  • Trade wars can also disrupt global supply chains
Increased costs
  • Tariffs on raw materials can increase production costs for downstream industries, making domestic products more expensive to produce
  • This leads to higher prices for consumers and can adversely affect the competitiveness of domestic industries reliant on imported intermediate goods
Higher prices
  • Protectionism leads to higher prices for domestic consumers, as tariffs and quotas can create shortages that drive up prices.
  • In some cases, high tariffs have led consumers to seek alternatives in neighbouring countries or contributed to the emergence of black markets for highly sought-after goods
Less choice
  • By limiting imports, protectionism restricts consumer choice, often leaving consumers with fewer options and potentially lower quality goods
  • International trade standards and protectionist policies can result in market dominance by a single product variety, reducing biodiversity and choice
Lack of incentive for domestic firms to become more efficient
  • Without the pressure of international competition, domestic firms may lack the motivation to innovate or seek more efficient production methods
  • This can lead to stagnation within industries, as firms rely on outdated technologies and processes instead of striving for improvement
Reduced export competitiveness
  • Protectionist policies can make domestic industries less competitive on the global stage by creating a high-cost environment
