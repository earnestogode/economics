Let's explore what some of the arguments are for trade protection

Protection of infant industries
  • Supports young sectors against international giants; Brazil's protection of its new industries as an example.
  • Pharmaceutical sector illustrates long R&D phases, high initial costs, and the need for economies of scale to lower unit costs.
  • Use of regulatory measures (like additional testing) to shield these industries, fostering domestic growth.
National security
  • Critical sectors like food and defense must be domestically produced for security; EU's agricultural subsidies to ensure food self-sufficiency.
  • Domestic production of defense equipment is vital to maintain sovereignty and security.
  • Protects the integrity and reliability of a nation's defense capabilities and food supply.
Maintenance of health and safety
  • Import standards safeguard consumers from harmful products; bans on items like the Kinder Surprise in the US
  • Ensures imported goods meet domestic health and safety criteria to protect public health.
  • Examples include mandatory ingredient listings on food products in the EU to maintain consumer safety
Environmental standard
  • Countries enforce production standards to mitigate environmental harm; UK's ban on microbeads in cosmetics.
  • Prevents pollution and protects biodiversity by restricting harmful substances.
  • Encourages sustainable practices and the use of environmentally friendly alternatives.
Anti-dumping
  • Addresses predatory pricing and dumping practices where goods are sold below cost to capture market share.
  • Protective tariffs and measures counter unfair competitive advantages gained through dumping.
  • Ensures fair competition and market stability by penalizing below-cost sales.
Unfair competition
  • Protects against intellectual property theft and ensures fair play in international trade.
  • Tariffs and restrictions help safeguard domestic innovators from global infringement.
  • Aims to maintain a level playing field for domestic firms against reverse-engineered products.
Balance-of-payments correction
  • Reduces imports to manage trade deficits and stabilize the national economy.
  • Aims to improve the country's financial position by adjusting trade policies.
  • Supports domestic industry growth, reducing dependency on foreign goods
Sources of government revenue
  • Tariffs as a vital revenue source for countries with limited taxation systems
  • Provides financial resources necessary for public services and infrastructure development.
  • Often crucial for economically least developed countries to fund government operations.
Protection of jobs
  • Trade protection safeguards jobs in industries vulnerable to international competition.
  • Examples include high tariffs on textiles in the USA to protect domestic employment.
  • Balances consumer costs against the economic benefit of maintaining employment within critical sectors.
Economically least developed country diversification
  • Temporary protection helps ELDCs develop a broad industrial base for future growth.
  • Encourages self-sufficiency and reduces reliance on imports by nurturing key sectors.
  • Aims for a diversified economy capable of producing a wide range of goods and services for both domestic use and export.
